    How the model tracks real sites

    The deployed PV model is run over the last ~90 days of real weather for each known site and compared against that site's measured output. This path never runs the weather model, so it isolates PV-model accuracy from weather-forecast error.

      About PV Prospect

      PV Prospect estimates the annual energy yield of a UK solar installation. The result is a typical-year climatological estimate — not a forecast for a specific year, but a long-run average learned from historical data.

      How the model works

      Two neural networks are chained with a physics step between them:

      1. Weather model — given location and time of year, predicts climatological values for direct normal irradiance (DNI), diffuse horizontal irradiance (DHI), and ambient temperature, from Open-Meteo historical data.
      2. Solar geometry — standard physics converts DNI and DHI into plane-of-array (POA) irradiance for the given panel orientation and tilt.
      3. PV model — predicts daily capacity factor from POA irradiance and temperature; a fixed degradation factor (~0.7 %/year) is applied for panel age. Trained on generation data from 10 real UK systems shared on PVOutput.

      Inputs

      • Location (latitude and longitude)
      • Panel rating (W) and optional inverter rating (W)
      • Orientation: azimuth (° clockwise from North) and tilt (° from horizontal)
      • Installation age (years, optional)

      Uncertainty

      The annual yield estimate carries a ±17 % (1σ) uncertainty floor, calibrated by leave-one-site-out cross-validation across the 10 training sites. It captures site-to-site variability but excludes weather-year fluctuations and real-world variation in shading, soiling, and roof condition. The training corpus is self-selected by motivated system owners, so estimates are optimistic for an arbitrary installation.
